Your Agriculture future awaits

Agriculture Technician Provide technical support to Agricultural Scientists by performing tests and experiments.

Successful individuals employed in agriculture occupations need a basic understanding of industry specific financial operations, goal-setting, human relations, crops, soil management, as well as livestock fundamentals.

Agriculture Technology

Farm machinery

The most common types of equipment and machinery used on farms include tractors, balers, combines, plows, mowers, planters and sprayers.

What's it like to be Agricultural Technician?

Agricultural Technicians are responsible for researching, developing and testing food and other agricultural products. They assist with setting up laboratory or field equipment required for testing and conduct experiments to improve the quality, resilience and yield of plants and animals.

Tasks and duties

  • Recording experiment data.
  • Collecting and collating research results.
  • Collecting animal and crop samples.
  • Supervising pest and weed control operations.
  • Conducting surveys of insect or plant diseases.
  • Measuring animal feed used in testing.
  • Planning production processes including slaughtering or harvesting.
  • Assisting in breeding plants and livestock to develop improved strains.
  • Preparing and presenting demonstrations in agricultural settings.
  • Assisting in preparing laboratory and field equipment for site testing.
